Who we are

A student‑run cultural organization connecting Bangladeshi students and friends of Bangladesh across Rowan University.

Rowan Bangladeshi Students Association (BSA) exists to celebrate Bangladeshi heritage while building a supportive community for students navigating life and academics at Rowan University.

We organize cultural programs, hangouts, and collaborative events that highlight Bangladeshi traditions, food, music, and history, while also helping new students adjust to campus and find their people.

What we do

Throughout the year, BSA hosts a mix of cultural, educational, and social programs, such as:

  • Cultural nights featuring Bangladeshi music, dance, and food.
  • Welcome and orientation sessions for new students.
  • Collaborations with other multicultural and international student organizations.
  • Study sessions, mentorship, and informal peer support.
  • Sports, game nights, and chill hangouts to de‑stress together.

Join Rowan BSA

Whether you grew up in Bangladesh or are just curious about the culture, you’re invited to be part of Rowan BSA.

Getting involved is simple — just show up to an event, say hi, and connect with us online so you don’t miss updates.

  • Stop by one of our events like the International Mother Language Month celebration.
  • Introduce yourself to any e‑board member — we’re always happy to welcome new faces.
  • Ask to be added to our GroupMe / mailing list for meeting and event reminders.

If you have questions, ideas for collaborations, or want to help out, feel free to reach out to the President or any board member by email.
